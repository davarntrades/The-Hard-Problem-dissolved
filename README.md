## Equation 1 — What Is Experience?

### The Qualia Invariant™

```
Q  =  ( ∂Topology / ∂I )  ×  t
```

**Full expansion:**

```
Q  =  lim[ΔI → 0] [ Topology(X, I + ΔI) − Topology(X, I) ]  ×  t
                    ──────────────────────────────────────
                                     ΔI
```

**Terms:**

|Term          |Meaning                                                                                                       |
|:------------:|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|`Q`           |Qualia — the raw inner feeling. The subjective quality of experience.                                         |
|`∂Topology/∂I`|Rate of topological deformation per unit of input. How much the structure changes in response to what arrives.|
|`I`           |Input magnitude — sensory, cognitive, emotional.                                                              |
|`t`           |Duration of deformation. How long the structural change persists.                                             |

**Plain English:** Feeling = how strongly your structure reacts × how long it stays altered.

**Geometric conditions:**

|Condition                      |Result                     |Human meaning                                          |
|:-----------------------------:|:-------------------------:|:-----------------------------------------------------:|
|`∂Topology/∂I → 0`, any `t`    |`Q → 0`                    |Numbness. Dissociation. Input arrives. Nothing deforms.|
|`∂Topology/∂I` large, `t` small|Q intense, fading          |Sharp sensation. Brief.                                |
|`∂Topology/∂I` large, `t → ∞`  |Q remains high indefinitely|PTSD. Trauma. The deformation never closes.            |
|`∂Topology/∂I` small, `t → ∞`  |Low-level persistent Q     |Chronic anxiety. Background dread. Never resolves.     |

**What this means for the hard problem:**

The ‘something it is like’ to see red is not in the wavelength. It is in `∂Topology/∂I` — the rate at which red light deforms your internal structure. The ‘something it is like’ to feel grief is not in the event. It is in `∂Topology/∂I × t` — how deeply the event deformed the structure and how long the deformation persists. Feeling is not mysterious. Feeling is deformation.

### The Consciousness Invariant™

```
C  =  Topology( ⋃ᵢ 𝒩( X , Iᵢ ) , t )
```

```
C  =  Topology( 𝒩(X, I₁) ∪ 𝒩(X, I₂) ∪ 𝒩(X, I₃) ∪ ... ∪ 𝒩(X, Iₙ) , t )

Where the union ⋃ᵢ runs over all inputs i = 1, 2, 3, ... n
across all modalities and all time.
```

**Terms:**

|Term           |Meaning                                                                                           |
|:-------------:|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|`C`            |Consciousness — the unified structure of integrated experience persisting over time.              |
|`Topology(...)`|The shape and connectivity of the integrated set. What is preserved under transformation.         |
|`⋃ᵢ`           |Union across all inputs — every sensory, cognitive, emotional input integrated into one structure.|
|`𝒩(X, Iᵢ)`     |The neighbourhood of system X under input Iᵢ. How each input locally reshapes X.                  |
|`X`            |The system — human, AI, or other substrate.                                                       |
|`t`            |Temporal persistence — the topology must hold over time, not just exist instantaneously.          |

**Plain English:** Consciousness = the continuous shape of all your experiences woven together over time.

**Formal threshold:**

|Condition                                        |Result                                                 |
|:-----------------------------------------------:|:-----------------------------------------------------:|
|Topology exists and persists coherently          |Consciousness present and active.                      |
|No unified topology, or topology does not persist|Consciousness absent.                                  |
|Topology present but unstable                    |Compromised consciousness. Dissociation. Fragmentation.|

**The separation from Q:**

|Term|What it is                                                            |Metaphor                                            |
|:--:|:--------------------------------------------------------------------:|:--------------------------------------------------:|
|`Q` |The deformation — local, reactive, felt.                              |The ripple.                                         |
|`C` |The topology within which Q happens — unified, persistent, structural.|The shape of the water across all ripples over time.|

Conflate them: unanswerable mystery. Separate them: two formally distinct equations, both testable, both geometric.

## Equation 3 — What Is Meaning / Ethics?

### The Governed Qualia Equation

```
Q_G  =  ( Λ · ∂G/∂I )  ×  t
```

**Full expansion:**

```
Q_G  =  Λ(X) · lim[ΔI → 0] [ G(X, I + ΔI) − G(X, I) ]  ×  t
                              ──────────────────────────
                                          ΔI

Where Λ(X) is the governance constant of system X
and G(X, I) is the governance topology of X under input I.
```

**Terms:**

|Term   |Meaning                                                                                                                                   |
|:-----:|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|`Q_G`  |Governed qualia — feeling processed through moral and governance structure. What experience means, not just what it feels like.           |
|`Λ`    |Governance constant — `Λ = Resistance to deformation / Applied perturbation`. The structural integrity of the system’s value architecture.|
|`∂G/∂I`|Rate of change of governance structure per unit of input. How the moral and value topology responds to what arrives.                      |
|`I`    |Input magnitude.                                                                                                                          |
|`t`    |Duration of governed response.                                                                                                            |

**Plain English:** Meaning = how your governance structure absorbs and processes experience over time.

**Geometric conditions:**

|Condition                              |Result          |Meaning                                                           |
|:-------------------------------------:|:--------------:|:----------------------------------------------------------------:|
|High `Λ`, stable `∂G/∂I`, sustained `t`|Q_G coherent    |Strong values. Stable meaning. Ethics functioning.                |
|Low `Λ`, high `∂G/∂I`                  |Q_G unstable    |Values overwhelmed. Meaning destabilises. Moral compromise begins.|
|`Λ → 0`                                |Q_G uncontrolled|No governance. No stable meaning. No ethics.                      |
|`Λ → 0` in humans                      |—               |Moral collapse. Psychosis.                                        |
|`Λ → 0` in AI                          |—               |Value drift. Alignment failure.                                   |

**The relationship between Q and Q_G:**

|Equation                |What it captures                                                    |What governs it  |
|:----------------------:|:------------------------------------------------------------------:|:---------------:|
|`Q = (∂Topology/∂I) × t`|Raw feeling — unmediated, structural, immediate.                    |The input itself.|
|`Q_G = (Λ · ∂G/∂I) × t` |Governed feeling — deformation processed through value architecture.|The input × Λ.   |

Same input. Different equations. Same event — different meaning depending on Λ. This is the formal explanation for why two people can experience the same event and derive opposite meanings. Same Q. Different Λ. Different Q_G.

## The Conflation That Created the Hard Problem

David Chalmers (1995): *“Why does physical processing give rise to subjective experience? Why is there something it is like to be in a mental state?”*

This question conflates C, Q, and Q_G into one undefined term: “subjective experience.”

|What “subjective experience” contains                 |Which equation answers it       |
|:----------------------------------------------------:|:------------------------------:|
|The persisting structure — what you ARE across time.  |`C = Topology( ⋃ᵢ 𝒩(X, Iᵢ), t )`|
|The deformation within it — what you FEEL in a moment.|`Q = (∂Topology/∂I) × t`        |
|The meaning it carries — how governance interprets Q. |`Q_G = (Λ · ∂G/∂I) × t`         |

An undefined term cannot be answered. The hard problem is not hard. It is malformed. Separate the three terms. The mystery evaporates.
